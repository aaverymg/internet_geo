NYC Public Wifi Hotspots

Kepler heatmap: link here

Data Courtesy NYC Open Data

For this project, I worked off of NYC Open Data’s list of publicly available wifi hotspots. This data shows every free public wifi hotspot in New York, with additional information like the provider. Using this data, I created a simple point map using Leaflet, and a heat map using Kepler. The Leaftlet map lets you examine individual points to see their exact location and provider, and the Kepler map shows patterns and trends in the placement of these hotspots. The heatmap is especially interesting to me, because the areas of highest concentration tend to correspond with major roads and subway lines. For the Leaflet map, if I were to process this data further, I would remove LinkNYC kiosk data points from the map- they make up the vast majority of the points, and I’m curious about the distribution of other kinds of publicly available wifi. The other common providers include Transit Wireless subway hotspots, public libraries, and wifi in public parks provided by major cell service companies like Spectrum and AT&T.
